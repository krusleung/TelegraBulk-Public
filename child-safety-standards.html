Child Safety Standards

TelegraBulk - Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Last Updated: October 22, 2025

Effective Date: October 22, 2025

ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY: TelegraBulk has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). Any use of our service in connection with CSAE is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate action including account termination and reporting to authorities.

1Our Commitment to Child Safety

TelegraBulk is committed to protecting children and preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation. We maintain strict standards and take proactive measures to ensure our platform is not used for any form of child endangerment.

1.1 Scope of These Standards

These standards apply to all users of TelegraBulk, including but not limited to:

  • All app users regardless of location
  • All content processed through the app
  • All communications facilitated by the app
  • Any use of our accessibility features

2Prohibited Activities

2.1 Strictly Prohibited

The following activities are explicitly prohibited and constitute immediate grounds for termination and reporting:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Sharing, transmitting, storing, or facilitating access to any CSAM
  • Child Exploitation: Any content that sexually exploits children
  • Child Grooming: Using the app to establish contact with minors for exploitative purposes
  • Child Trafficking: Any content related to human trafficking of minors
  • Inappropriate Content Targeting Minors: Sexually suggestive content involving minors
  • Solicitation: Soliciting CSAM or attempting to contact minors inappropriately

2.2 Zero Tolerance Enforcement

TelegraBulk enforces a zero-tolerance policy. Any violation will result in:

  • Immediate termination of service access
  • Preservation of evidence
  • Report to National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • Report to appropriate law enforcement agencies
  • Full cooperation with investigations

3Detection and Prevention

3.1 User Reporting Mechanism

We provide multiple ways for users to report concerns:

Report Child Safety Concerns:

  • Email (24/7): cs@krusleung.com
  • Subject Line: "Child Safety Report - URGENT"
  • In-App: Via feedback form in app settings

Response Time: All child safety reports are treated as highest priority and reviewed within 24 hours.

3.2 Proactive Measures

While TelegraBulk processes data locally and does not monitor message content, we:

  • Clearly communicate prohibited use in our Terms of Service
  • Provide accessible reporting mechanisms
  • Take immediate action on all reports
  • Maintain records for law enforcement cooperation
  • Regularly review and update our child safety procedures

4Response Procedures

4.1 Upon Receipt of Report

When we receive a child safety report, we immediately:

  1. Priority Review: Escalate to designated child safety contact within 1 hour
  2. Initial Assessment: Review reported content/user within 24 hours
  3. Preservation: Preserve all relevant information and evidence
  4. Action: Take immediate action against violating users/content

4.2 CSAM Response Protocol

Upon obtaining actual knowledge of CSAM:

  1. Immediate Removal: Remove or disable access to content immediately
  2. Preserve Evidence: Maintain records for law enforcement
  3. NCMEC Report: File report with National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) via CyberTipline
  4. Law Enforcement: Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations
  5. Account Termination: Permanently ban users involved

4.3 Reporting Timeline

  • NCMEC Report: Within 24 hours of confirmed CSAM
  • Content Removal: Immediate (within hours of confirmation)
  • User Notification: Law enforcement consulted before notifying user to avoid evidence destruction

6Cooperation with Authorities

6.1 Law Enforcement Partnership

TelegraBulk is committed to full cooperation with:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
  • Local law enforcement agencies
  • International law enforcement (INTERPOL, Europol, etc.)
  • Child protection organizations

6.2 Information Disclosure

We will disclose user information to law enforcement when:

  • Legally required by valid court order or subpoena
  • Necessary to protect child safety
  • Required for CSAM reporting to NCMEC
  • Requested by law enforcement in child endangerment cases
Note: TelegraBulk processes all data locally on user devices and does not operate servers that store user content. However, we maintain user account information and will provide available information to law enforcement when legally required or necessary for child protection.

7User Responsibilities

7.1 User Obligations

All TelegraBulk users must:

  • Not use the app for any illegal purpose, especially child endangerment
  • Report suspected CSAE activity immediately
  • Comply with all applicable laws regarding child protection
  • Use the app responsibly and ethically

7.2 Age Restrictions

TelegraBulk requires users to:

  • Be at least 13 years of age (or applicable age in your jurisdiction)
  • Comply with Messaging App's age requirements (13+ or 16+ in EU)
  • Not use the app on behalf of minors for prohibited purposes

8Training and Awareness

TelegraBulk maintains awareness of child safety issues through:

  • Regular review of NCMEC resources and guidelines
  • Monitoring of child safety best practices
  • Updates to policies in line with legal requirements
  • Staff training on identifying and responding to CSAE concerns

9Technical Safeguards

9.1 Platform Design

TelegraBulk's architecture inherently limits CSAE risks:

  • No Content Hosting: We don't store or host user messages or images
  • No User-to-User Communication: No in-app messaging between users
  • No Content Sharing Platform: Not a social network or file sharing service
  • Local Processing Only: All data remains on user devices

9.2 Third-Party Integration

Messages are sent through Messaging App's infrastructure:

  • Subject to Messaging App's own child safety policies and detection
  • Monitored by Messaging App's safety systems
  • Users must comply with Messaging App Terms of Service

10Reporting to NCMEC

10.1 CyberTipline Reporting

Upon obtaining actual knowledge of CSAM, we will:

  • File a report with NCMEC via CyberTipline (www.cybertipline.org)
  • Include all required information:
    • Description of CSAM content
    • User information (if available)
    • Timestamp and circumstances of discovery
    • Any other relevant evidence
  • Preserve evidence for law enforcement
  • Cooperate with any follow-up investigation

10.2 Legal Compliance

Our NCMEC reporting complies with:

  • 18 U.S.C. § 2258A (Reporting requirements)
  • 18 U.S.C. § 2252 (CSAM laws)
  • International child protection laws in jurisdictions we operate
